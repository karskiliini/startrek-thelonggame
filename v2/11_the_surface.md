# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 11 — "THE SURFACE"


EXT. CORDIA IV — BEAM-DOWN SITE — DAY


Kirk, Hummer, and a small away team materialize on the surface.

The beam-down site is in the Federation administrative zone — the official one. A landing pad. A building with the Federation seal. The seal is clean. The building is empty.

No one meets them.

Kirk looks around. The administrative zone is a ghost. Offices closed. Walkways empty. A flag that no one salutes.

Beyond it — the colony. And the colony is alive. Loud. Dense. The streets packed with people, commerce, vehicles, noise.


EXT. CORDIA IV — CARDASSIAN COMMERCIAL DISTRICT — CONTINUOUS


They walk into the colony proper.

The Cardassian district is first — because the Cardassian district is everything now. What was once a neighbourhood has become a city. Multi-level buildings. A market that stretches for blocks. The smell of food — real food, not replicated, cooked in the open air. Cardassian script everywhere. Music from somewhere.

It is, by any measure, a functioning civilization. Better than functioning — thriving.

And the human sector is visible at the edges. Smaller. Dimmer. The prefab walls from Scene 1. The streets where the lights go out.


                         HUMMER
               (to Kirk, quiet)
          The Cardassian zone has tripled since I was here. The human sector has halved.

They walk. Through the market. Past a Cardassian medical clinic — new, well-equipped, a line of patients outside. Past a school where children are learning in Cardassian. Past an intersection where a Cardassian traffic officer directs movement with the casual authority of a man who has been doing this for years.

Nobody looks at Kirk's uniform with hostility. Nobody looks at it with deference either. They look at it the way you look at a tourist.

A Klingon patrol at the boundary of the Klingon zone. Armed. The border between the Cardassian district and the Klingon district is as sharp as a knife edge. One step — Cardassian market, warm lights, commerce. Next step — Klingon territory, industrial, grey, functional.


                         HUMMER
          Three cultures. Three governments. No coordination.


                         KIRK
          Where's the Federation authority?


                         HUMMER
          You're standing on it, Captain.

Kirk looks down. The pavement under his feet. Federation-issued modular flooring, installed thirty years ago, cracked and patched with Cardassian repair material.


EXT. CORDIA IV — HUMAN SECTOR — LATER


The human sector.

Kirk sees it. What the viewer couldn't show. What the sensor data only implied.

The streets from Scene 1 — in daylight now. The prefab buildings. The patched roofing. The power cables. But also: people. Humans. Living here. Children playing in a courtyard with a broken fountain. An old man sitting outside a shop that sells nothing anyone seems to want.

The contrast with the Cardassian district two hundred meters away is not subtle. It is violence made architectural. The same planet, the same sky, and two completely different worlds separated by a street.

A woman recognizes the Starfleet uniform. She stops. Stares.


                         WOMAN
          You're Federation.

Kirk nods.


                         WOMAN (CONT'D)
          We haven't seen Federation uniforms in — I don't know how long.

               (beat — then, harder)

          What took you so long?

Kirk doesn't have an answer. He has orders. Orders are not answers.

The woman turns away. Goes back inside.


                         HUMMER
               (very quiet)
          Vega was tough. This is ten times worse.


                         KIRK
          I know.

He looks at the Cardassian skyline rising above the human sector. The buildings that Marek built. The schools. The hospitals. The infrastructure that works — for some. And the darkness that falls every night on the people it doesn't work for.

Kirk has the orders in his pocket. Terminate the agreement. Tell forty million people to leave.

He looks at the human sector. The people who would cheer.

He looks at the Cardassian district. The people who built everything.

Grey water. Nothing is clean. Nothing is simple.

He taps his communicator.


                         KIRK
          Kirk to Enterprise. I need the location of the senior Cardassian representative on the surface. Gul Marek.


                         SPOCK (V.O.)
          Gul Marek maintains a private compound in the eastern residential quarter. I am transmitting coordinates.

               (beat)

          Captain. You have been invited to dinner.
