# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 1 — "THE DARK"


EXT. SPACE — CORDIA IV — NIGHT SIDE


A planet from orbit. The night side. City lights visible — but unevenly. One hemisphere glows with the organized geometry of infrastructure that works. Roads, power grids, the clean bright lines of investment.

The other hemisphere is dimmer. Scattered. A patchwork of light clusters thinning toward the edges like a fire burning out.

We push in. Past the atmosphere. Past the clouds. Down.


EXT. CORDIA IV — HUMAN COLONY SECTOR — NIGHT


Streets. Low buildings. A settlement that was built to be temporary thirty years ago and never stopped being temporary. Prefab walls. Patched roofing. Power cables strung between structures like veins on the back of a hand.

People. Human. Going about the particular business of a community that has learned to do everything before dark.

A market closing down. A vendor pulling tarps over produce. Children being called inside — not with the casual rhythm of bedtime, but with urgency. The specific urgency of a place where night means something different than it does on Earth.

A woman locks a door. Checks it. Checks it again.

Somewhere — shouting. Cardassian. Then silence.

The power grid flickers. Not a surge — a test. Someone touching the switch to see if anyone reacts.

Nobody reacts. They've learned not to.

Then — the lights go out.

Not all at once. Block by block. The organized dimming of a grid being shut down from somewhere else. The street lights die first. Then the buildings. Then the market. Then the homes.

Darkness rolls through the human sector like a tide.

In the dark — sounds. Footsteps. Something heavy. The particular silence of people who are holding their breath inside locked doors, waiting for it to pass.

A child's voice. Muffled. Silenced by a hand.

The stars above Cordia are bright. The Cardassian sector on the far side of the settlement line still glows. The Klingon mining operations in the hills still pulse with industrial light.

The human sector is dark.


TITLE CARD:

STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME


The title holds over the darkness. Then fades.

The stars remain.
