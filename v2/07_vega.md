# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 7 — "VEGA"


EXT. SPACE — VEGA SYSTEM — ESTABLISHING


Enterprise drops out of warp. Vega Colony. Not a garden world like Arctura — a harder place. Rocky. Industrial. The kind of planet people go to because they can't afford to go somewhere better.

From orbit it looks functional. Not beautiful. The settlements are denser, older, less organized. The infrastructure grid has the particular look of something that was built piece by piece over decades without a plan.


INT. ENTERPRISE-A — BRIDGE


Hummer is at tactical. His face hasn't changed. But his hands have stopped moving.

He is looking at his home.


                         SPOCK
          Vega Colony. Federation registry. Population one hundred twelve thousand. Communications have been intermittent for eight months.

               (beat)

          We are being hailed. Standard frequency.

Kirk glances at Hummer. One second. The question he doesn't ask: are you alright?

Hummer's hands resume moving. That is his answer.


                         KIRK
          On screen.

A COLONIAL OFFICIAL appears. Not the polished administrator of Arctura. A tired woman in a uniform that hasn't been pressed. Behind her — an office that looks like it hasn't been cleaned in weeks.


                         COLONIAL OFFICIAL
          Enterprise. We weren't expecting a starship.

               (not hostile — exhausted)

          What do you need?


                         KIRK
          We're investigating communication disruptions in the sector. Your reports have been irregular.


                         COLONIAL OFFICIAL
          Our subspace relay has been — we've filed maintenance requests. Seven of them.

               (beat)

          The relay works fine for Cardassian commercial traffic. Just not for us.


EXT. VEGA COLONY — STREETS — DAY


Kirk, Spock, Hummer. On the surface.

It is immediately different from Arctura.

On Arctura the Cardassian presence was polite. Integrated. The good school, the better replicators, the friendly neighbours.

On Vega the Cardassian presence is dominant.

A main street. Cardassian commercial signs in primary position — Federation Standard beneath, smaller. A Cardassian trade office occupying what was clearly once a Federation administrative building. The Federation seal is still visible on the wall — behind the Cardassian signage, half-covered.

Cardassian officials moving through the street with the posture of people who own it. Not aggressive. Worse — indifferent. The particular indifference of a ruling class that no longer needs to assert itself because the assertion is complete.

A human vendor at a stall. A Cardassian administrator walks past without looking at her. She moves her goods slightly to make room. Automatic. The gesture of a person who has learned to take up less space.

Hummer sees this. His jaw tightens.


                         HUMMER
               (very quiet, to Kirk)
          That's new.


                         KIRK
          The Cardassians?


                         HUMMER
          The way they walk.

He's right. It isn't the walk of guests or workers or partners. It's the walk of owners.

They pass a junction. A Klingon patrol — four soldiers, armed, walking the perimeter of what is clearly a Klingon sector. The boundary is invisible but absolute. The architecture changes. The people change. The sound changes.


                         SPOCK
          The colony appears to be divided into de facto administrative zones. Cardassian, Klingon, and Federation sectors operating under separate informal authority structures.


                         KIRK
          Informal.


                         SPOCK
          No formal partition exists in any Federation record. The colony charter designates a unified administration under Federation colonial law.

               (beat)

          That charter does not appear to be in effect.


A FEDERATION OFFICER — not a colonial official, a Starfleet lieutenant posted here — finds them. Young. Thin. The particular look of someone who has been alone at their post for too long.


                         FEDERATION OFFICER
          Captain Kirk. I — we've been filing reports, sir.


                         KIRK
          I know.


                         FEDERATION OFFICER
          Nobody reads them.


                         KIRK
          I'm reading them now.

The officer looks at Kirk the way a drowning man looks at a boat. Relief and fury in equal measure — relief that someone came, fury that it took this long.


                         FEDERATION OFFICER
          Sir, the Cardassians have effective control of seventy percent of the colony's infrastructure. Water, power, communications. The subspace relay goes down when they want it down. Our maintenance requests go to a Cardassian dispatch office.

               (beat)

          We've been here for six years and they act like we're the guests.


EXT. VEGA COLONY — HUMAN SECTOR — LATER


Kirk and Hummer. Walking alone. Spock has gone to take sensor readings.

The human sector. Smaller. Older. The buildings that were here before the Cardassians came. Patched walls. Tired infrastructure. The same prefab aesthetic as the Cordia settlement in Scene 1 — temporary structures that became permanent through neglect.

Hummer is quiet. He's looking at streets he walked as a child. The market where his mother bought food. The corner where he learned that some problems don't wait for you to be old enough to solve them.


                         HUMMER
          It wasn't like this when I left.

               (beat)

          It was rough. It was always rough. But it was ours. People complained about each other, not about —

He gestures. The Cardassian commercial district visible at the end of the street, gleaming. The contrast is obscene.


                         HUMMER (CONT'D)
          When did this happen?


                         KIRK
          Gradually. Then all at once.

Kirk looks at Hummer. Hummer looks at the street he grew up on. Neither of them says what they're both thinking: Cordia will be worse.


                         KIRK (CONT'D)
          You alright?


                         HUMMER
          I'm fine, Captain.

He isn't fine. Kirk knows it. Hummer knows Kirk knows it. Neither of them needs to say more.

They walk back toward the beam-out point. Past the Cardassian sector with its clean streets and new buildings. Past the Klingon perimeter with its armed patrols. Past the Federation flag on the old administrative building, half-hidden behind signage in a language that isn't theirs.
