# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 10 — "GREY WATER"


EXT. SPACE — CORDIA SYSTEM


Enterprise drops out of warp.

The system opens on the viewer. The planet — green and blue, still beautiful, still looking cared for. The moon with its dark canyon wound. And beyond it, the asteroid belt. A river of ancient rock.

HUMMER, at the tactical station, stops what he's doing.

He looks at the viewer.

He's been here before. Twelve years ago. In a shuttle with bad lights and a failing power cell. He cut rock out of that belt for two years. He ate bad food in a colony that was falling apart. He left when he could.

Now the colony is flourishing. From orbit you can see the organized geometry of infrastructure that works — roads, power distribution, the clean lines of something someone invested in.

And at the edges — dimmer. A cluster of settlements to the northwest where the light grid thins to almost nothing. The same planet, the same night side, but the brightness drops like a border drawn in electricity. The human sector. The sector from Scene 1.

He looks at it for a long moment. Too long for a tactical officer on duty.


                         HUMMER
               (barely audible, to himself)
          That's not the same place.

Kirk, from the command chair, notices the pause. Files it. Says nothing.

Enterprise still has her standard procedures — the ones she follows even on special assignments, as long as they don't interfere with the mission. This time the standard procedure and the mission happen to want the same thing.


                         SPOCK
               (reading his console — the tone of pure data)
          Captain. The colony registry lists the current population as approximately twelve million.

               (beat)

          Biosensor readings from orbit indicate an actual population of approximately forty million.

A beat. Nobody responds.


                         KIRK
          Forty million.


                         SPOCK
          Correct. A discrepancy of twenty-eight million unregistered residents. The breakdown: sixty-one percent Cardassian nationals. Twenty-three percent Klingon. Sixteen percent Federation-origin.

Sixteen percent. On a Federation colony. Their colony. Sixteen percent of forty million is roughly six million humans. Surrounded.


                         SPOCK (CONT'D)
          The settlement naming conventions on the northern and eastern grids are Cardassian. The commercial frequencies are predominantly Cardassian. The subspace relay is routing through a Cardassian communications hub.

               (beat)

          That would explain the communication disruption.


                         KIRK
          Status report, Mr. Hummer.

Hummer snaps back. Professional. But something behind his eyes hasn't quite returned to the console.


                         HUMMER
          Klingon warbird in high orbit. IKS Rek'thar — Commander K'Tagh commanding.

               (beat)

          The Dread.

He pauses.


                         HUMMER (CONT'D)
          Weapons are hot, Captain.


                         KIRK
          Yellow alert.

No hesitation. The words are out before Hummer finishes the sentence. The bridge shifts — not panic, but the particular tightening of a crew that has just been reminded they are on a warship.


                         HUMMER
          Captain — the Rek'thar is breaking orbit. Moving to intercept. She's putting herself between us and the planet.

On the viewer — the warbird. Moving with purpose. Not fast. Deliberate. Sliding into position between Enterprise and Cordia like a door being closed.


                         KIRK
          Open a channel.


                         COMMUNICATIONS OFFICER
          No response, Captain. They're not answering hails.

The Rek'thar holds position. Weapons charged. Between Enterprise and the planet. The message is physical, not verbal.


                         BONES
          He's not going to let us through.


                         KIRK
          He doesn't have to let us through. We have the right to orbit a Federation colony.

               (to Hummer)

          Take us in. Standard approach vector. Slow.


                         HUMMER
          Captain — their weapons are locked.


                         KIRK
          Noted. Continue approach.

Enterprise moves forward. Slowly. The Rek'thar grows on the viewer. The gap narrows. Every officer on the bridge is calculating the distance — the firing solution, the shield response time, the margin of error.

A channel opens. K'TAGH on the viewer. He is standing too close to the screen. The face fills the frame.


                         K'TAGH
          Kirk.

Not Captain Kirk. Not a greeting. A sound.


                         K'TAGH (CONT'D)
          You brought a big ship to a small problem. That is either stupid or insulting. Which is it?


                         KIRK
          Commander. I'm here under orders to re-establish contact with the Federation colony. Standard procedure.


                         K'TAGH
          Standard procedure. Yes.

He grins. The grin of a man who has heard a lie and appreciates the craftsmanship.


                         K'TAGH (CONT'D)
          I know who you are, Kirk. I read your file. I read all of it. You are the man who does not create complications. Until he does.

               (beat)

          I am not going to be surprised, Kirk.


                         KIRK
          I have no intention of surprising anyone, Commander. Standard orbit. Routine assessment.

K'Tagh stares at him through the screen. Five seconds. Six.

Then the Rek'thar moves. Slowly. Pulling aside. Not retreating — repositioning. The warbird heads toward the asteroid belt, settling into position above the Klingon mining sector.

Kirk watches. The gap opens. The planet is clear.

But the feeling on the bridge is not relief. It is the feeling of walking through a door that someone held open — knowing they can close it behind you.


                         BONES
          Well that was awkward.


                         HUMMER
          Weapons are powering down. She's holding station over the Klingon mining sector.


                         KIRK
          Stand down yellow alert.

The bridge exhales.


                         KIRK (CONT'D)
          Standard orbit. Begin full sensor sweep. Mr. Spock — I want the belt survey we discussed. Full spectral analysis.

               (beat)

          Senior staff briefing when the initial results are in. I'll be in my quarters.

He stands. Pauses at the turbolift.

Below him — through the viewer — the planet. Beautiful from orbit. Sixty-one percent Cardassian. Sixteen percent human. And somewhere down there, in a sector where the lights go out at night, Federation citizens living in the dark.

He has orders in his pocket that will change everything.

He goes to his quarters to read them one more time.
