# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 13 — "THE CLOCK"


INT. ENTERPRISE-A — BRIDGE


Kirk returns from the surface. Dust on his boots. Something in his face that wasn't there when he left.

The bridge crew reads him the way a crew reads a captain — not the words but the posture, the walk, the particular set of the jaw that means something happened down there that he's still processing.


                         SPOCK
          Captain. The belt survey has produced results.

Kirk looks at the tactical display. The asteroid belt — and seven amber dots glowing in the Klingon sector.


                         SPOCK (CONT'D)
          Seven thermal anomalies in the inner ring. Heat signatures consistent with subsurface installations. Power generation, atmospheric processing. The largest is approximately seventy percent operational.

               (beat)

          They are not mining facilities.


                         KIRK
          What are they?


                         SPOCK
          At this resolution, I cannot determine their specific purpose. The mineral composition of the surrounding rock degrades our sensors. A closer survey would be required.


                         KIRK
          Inside the belt.


                         SPOCK
          Affirmative.

Kirk looks at the amber dots. Looks at the planet below. Looks at the Rek'thar, holding position above the Klingon mining sector.


                         BONES
          Jim. How did it go down there?


                         KIRK
          Marek isn't leaving. He was very clear about that.


                         BONES
          And the order?


                         KIRK
          Delivered.


                         BONES
          So we've told a man who built a country that he has to abandon it, and he's said no.


                         KIRK
          That's about the size of it.


                         BONES
          And the people in the human sector? The ones living in the dark?


                         KIRK
          They asked me what took us so long.

A silence on the bridge. The particular silence of a crew that is beginning to understand there are no good options.


                         HUMMER
          Captain.

Something in his voice. Kirk turns.


                         HUMMER (CONT'D)
          Long-range sensors. Klingon cruiser on approach vector. IKS Vor'nak. Commander Toral.

The bridge goes cold.


                         HUMMER (CONT'D)
          ETA — fourteen hours.

Fourteen hours. Enterprise is inside the system. The Rek'thar is at the belt. And a Klingon cruiser is coming. In fourteen hours there will be two Klingon warships and one Enterprise.


                         BONES
          Jim.

The doctor's voice. The voice that says what Kirk is thinking.


                         BONES (CONT'D)
          We have four hundred people on this ship. Fourteen hours.

               (beat)

          We can still get out. One Klingon warbird at the belt — we go now, we make it past. Fourteen hours from now, we don't.


                         KIRK
          And the colony?


                         BONES
          The colony has been here for thirty years without us. It'll survive fourteen more hours.


                         KIRK
          The human sector won't. Not if we leave. Marek knows Enterprise is the only thing between his operation and complete control. The moment we leave, Federation authority on this planet ends. Permanently.

               (beat)

          Sixteen percent, Bones. That's the Federation population on a Federation colony. If we leave now — there won't be a Federation population when we come back.


                         BONES
          If we stay — there might not be an Enterprise to come back with.

The argument that has no answer. Four hundred on the ship against thousands on the ground. The mathematics of duty.

Kirk looks at the tactical display. Seven amber dots in the belt. A Klingon warbird. A cruiser coming. A colony where lights go out at night.


                         KIRK
               (to Hummer)
          Mr. Hummer. The belt. Those seven installations. How close would you need to get for a detailed scan?


                         HUMMER
          Close. Inside the ring. A shuttle with modified sensors could do it — if someone knew the belt well enough to navigate the inner corridors.


                         KIRK
          You know those corridors.


                         HUMMER
          I cut rock in them for two years, Captain.

Kirk nods. The nod of a man who has made his decision. Not the decision to stay — that was made the moment Vor'nak appeared on the sensors. He was never going to leave. The decision is what to do with the fourteen hours he has left.


                         KIRK
          Take a mining shuttle. Full sensor package. Get inside those asteroids and find out what the Klingons are building. You have twelve hours — I want you back before that cruiser arrives.

               (to the bridge)

          Standard orbit. All departments, full readiness. Senior staff briefing when Mr. Hummer's survey is complete. I'll be in my quarters.

               (beat — to no one in particular)

          We stay.
