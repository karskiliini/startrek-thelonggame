# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 12 — "THE COOK"


EXT. CORDIA IV — MAREK'S COMPOUND — EVENING


A walled compound in the eastern quarter. The wall is not high — not a fortress. A garden wall. The kind that says: this is private, not: this is defended. Flowering plants that are not native to this world.

Kirk approaches. Alone. He left the away team at the beam-down site. This is not a meeting that should have witnesses.

A door opens before he reaches it. A Cardassian attendant. Polite. No weapons visible. The entrance to the compound smells like food. Real food. Oil and heat and something animal.


INT. MAREK'S COMPOUND — DINING ROOM — CONTINUOUS


The room is warm. The room has been warm for hours.

A table set for two. A bottle of Kanar at the center — dark, slightly luminescent. Candlelight. The particular intimacy of a space that has been prepared by someone who understands that meals are performances and has decided to give his best one.

GUL MAREK stands at the far end, carrying a plate. An apron — old, heavy, leather, knives in sheaths along the front. He is somewhere in his fifties. A Cardassian of the old school — the neck ridges and bone structure giving him the quality of looking simultaneously civilian and military regardless of what he wears. He wears civilian clothes tonight. They fit him like a uniform.

He sets the plate down and looks up. The warmth is immediate. Genuine. The smile of a host who has been waiting for exactly this moment.


                         MAREK
          Captain Kirk. Welcome to my home.

The word home. On a Federation colony. In a compound he built, furnished, and cooked in as though it were his kitchen, his table, his house. He has attendants, a garden, and a supply chain that stretches across two sectors for one animal.


                         MAREK (CONT'D)
          I apologize for the delay — the taska was prepared yesterday, but the final sear is temperamental. A specific heat for the first four minutes or the protein seizes.

               (genuine warmth)

          Please. Sit.

Kirk sits. He looks at the room. The food. The bottle. The man in the apron with knives.


                         MAREK (CONT'D)
          The taska was transported live from Cardassia Prime. It does not survive replication — something in the protein structure. I have a friend in the agricultural ministry who arranges these things.


                         KIRK
          That's a long supply chain for one meal.


                         MAREK
          Everything worth eating requires a long supply chain, Captain. That is as true for civilizations as it is for dinner.

He pours the Kanar. The movements of a host, not a guest. He is feeding Kirk — literally, personally, from his own hands — on a Federation colony.

Kirk eats. It is, genuinely, extraordinary.


                         KIRK
          It's very good.


                         MAREK
          Everything on this colony is better than it was when the Federation was running it alone. This is not arrogance. It is simply a fact.

He settles into his chair with the controlled comfort of a man who has prepared for this meal the way other men prepare for battle.


                         MAREK (CONT'D)
          Captain, I know why you're here. You're here because the communications stopped and someone in San Francisco noticed. I'm impressed it only took six weeks.

               (beat — not hostile, almost amused)

          The communications relay is fine, Captain. It works perfectly. For everyone who is running this colony. The disruption was — let us call it a prioritization issue. Cardassian administrative traffic takes precedence. There is a great deal of administrative traffic.

Kirk puts down his fork.


                         KIRK
          You blocked Federation communications from a Federation colony.


                         MAREK
          I prioritized essential communications in a colony that I have built, staffed, and maintained for thirty years. Federation administrative traffic is not essential to the daily operation of this colony. It has not been essential for some time.

               (the smile — warm, genuine, and absolutely certain)

          Captain. I built the hospitals where your citizens receive medical care. I built the schools where their children learn. I built the power grid, the water supply, the roads. The food they eat comes from systems I designed. Sixty-one percent of the people on this planet are Cardassian nationals who came here to build something — and they built it.

He picks up his glass. The Kanar catches the candlelight.


                         MAREK (CONT'D)
          Your people are welcome here. They have always been welcome. But the suggestion that this is a Federation colony in anything but name — that is a conversation I have been waiting to have for a very long time.


                         KIRK
          Then let's have it.

Kirk reaches into his jacket. Removes a PADD. Sets it on the table between them — between the Kanar and the plates and the remnants of a meal prepared by a man who has been cooking for thirty years.


                         KIRK (CONT'D)
          Gul Marek. By order of the Federation Council, the Cardassian development agreement at Cordia IV is terminated. Effective immediately. All Cardassian administrative, commercial, and infrastructure operations are to cease within a timeframe to be negotiated.

               (beat)

          I'm sorry.

The room is very quiet.

Marek looks at the PADD. He doesn't touch it.

He looks at Kirk. The warmth is gone. Not replaced by anger — by something older. The particular stillness of a man who has spent thirty years building a house and has just been told to leave it.


                         MAREK
          Negotiated.


                         KIRK
          That's what the order says.


                         MAREK
          Captain Kirk. I have three hundred thousand people on this planet. Families. Children who were born here. They have never known another home. This is not an agreement that can be terminated. This is a civilization.

               (beat — very quiet)

          We are not leaving.

The sentence sits on the table like the PADD. Final. Absolute.


                         KIRK
          The order is from the Federation Council. It's not a request.


                         MAREK
          And my answer is not a negotiating position. We built this colony. We are this colony. If the Federation wants us to leave — the Federation will have to remove us.

               (he picks up his glass — the steadiness of the hand is deliberate)

          Do you have the stomach for that, Captain? Does the Federation?

Kirk looks at him. The man who cooked him dinner. The man who built hospitals and schools and a civilization. The man who blocked communications to a Federation colony and considers it "prioritization."

He has no answer. Because both things are true. The Cardassians built everything. And the Cardassians took everything.


                         KIRK
          The order stands, Gul. I'll be in touch regarding the timeline.


                         MAREK
          There will be no timeline, Captain. There will only be this: we are here. We have always been here. And we will be here when your ship is gone.

Kirk stands. The meal is over. The PADD sits on the table, untouched by Marek's hands.

Kirk leaves.

Marek sits alone at his table. The food. The Kanar. The PADD with the order that ends thirty years.

He does not throw the apron. Not yet. He is still a diplomat. He is still a cook. He is still a man who believes he can win this with patience and infrastructure and the particular Cardassian genius for making occupation look like generosity.

Not yet.

But the meal is cold. And for the first time in thirty years, Marek is not hungry.
