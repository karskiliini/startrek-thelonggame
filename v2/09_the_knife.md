# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 9 — "THE KNIFE"


INT. ENTERPRISE-A — CAPTAIN'S QUARTERS — NIGHT


Enterprise at warp. Hours from Cordia.

Kirk is alone. The lights are low. A cup of something on his desk that he hasn't touched.

A chime. Not the door — the communications console. Priority channel. Encrypted. The kind that arrives with its own silence.


                         KIRK
          Kirk here.


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.)
          Captain. Secure channel confirmed.

Her voice is flat. Professional. The particular flatness that Kirk has learned to associate with orders he won't want to hear.


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.) (CONT'D)
          The Federation Council has reviewed the intelligence assessments from the border sector. Your reports from Arctura III and the Vega system have been received and incorporated.

               (beat)

          The Council has reached a decision regarding the Cordia colony.


                         KIRK
          I'm listening.


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.)
          The Cardassian development agreement at Cordia IV is to be terminated. Effective immediately upon delivery. All Cardassian administrative, commercial, and infrastructure operations are to cease within a timeframe to be negotiated on the ground.

               (beat)

          You are to deliver this notification to the senior Cardassian representative at Cordia. In person. On the surface.

Kirk is quiet.

He has seen Arctura III — where the Cardassians built the best school in the sector.

He has seen Vega — where they own the streets and the humans have learned to take up less space.

He knows what Cordia is. Hummer told him. Three cultures colliding. Violence every night. Anarchy.

And now he is being sent to tell three hundred thousand people — people who built schools, hospitals, roads, an entire civilization from nothing — that their agreement is over. That they need to leave.


                         KIRK
          Admiral. There are three hundred thousand Cardassian nationals at Cordia. Many of them have been there for decades. Families. Children born on that planet.


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.)
          The Council is aware of the demographics.


                         KIRK
          Are they aware of what termination means on the ground? These people built that colony. The infrastructure doesn't exist without them. If we tell them to leave —


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.)
          Captain. The intelligence is clear. The Cardassian presence at Cordia is not a development partnership. It is a strategic operation that has achieved de facto control of a Federation colony. The pattern is consistent across the sector. You've seen it yourself.

               (beat)

          The Council's decision is final. Enterprise is to deliver the notification and remain on station to oversee the transition.


                         KIRK
          And if they don't leave?

A silence. The silence of a question that has been asked in a room Kirk can't see, by people he'll never meet, and answered with the particular vagueness that allows institutions to avoid saying what they mean.


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.)
          The Council is confident that diplomatic channels will be sufficient.


                         KIRK
          With respect, Admiral — the diplomatic channels on the ground don't exist anymore. I've seen what happens to Federation authority in these colonies. There is no one to negotiate with. The Cardassians ARE the authority.


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.)
          That is precisely why the agreement is being terminated, Captain.

               (beat — then, quieter, almost human)

          Jim. I know what this is. I know what it costs. But the alternative is losing the colony permanently — and every colony after it.


                         KIRK
          Understood, Admiral.


                         ADMIRAL VOSS (V.O.)
          Voss out.

The channel closes.

Kirk sits in the dark.

The PADD on his desk. The orders. The words that will end thirty years of Cardassian presence on a planet they built from nothing.

He thinks about the school on Arctura III. The children playing outside. Cardassian children and human children together.

He thinks about the vendor on Vega who moved her goods to make room.

He thinks about Hummer saying: the human sector is what's left over.

He picks up the PADD. His hand trembles. He sets it down.

He picks it up again.

He reads the orders one more time. Then he stands. Goes to the viewport. Stars at warp. Cordia somewhere ahead in the dark.

He is about to walk into a colony of three hundred thousand people and tell them that the Federation — the institution that couldn't keep its own flagship's water clean, that let its border colonies disappear one by one into someone else's hands, that filed and ignored reports for fifteen years — has decided that enough is enough.

The timing is not coincidental. The Federation waited until it had lost almost everything before acting. And now it is sending one man, on one ship, to deliver the consequences of its own neglect.

Kirk knows this.

He goes anyway.
