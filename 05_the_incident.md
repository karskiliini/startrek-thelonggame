# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 5 — "THE INCIDENT"

---

`INT. ENTERPRISE-A — BRIDGE — LATER`

The shift has changed. Quieter now.

HUMMER at tactical. The readings scrolling across his console. He's been running the same analysis for twenty minutes.

The explosion happens before he can decide what it means.

`CUT TO:`

---

`EXT. KERATA IV — COLONY SURFACE — CONTINUOUS`

A building in the eastern residential sector. The blast is large but contained — the specific character of an explosion that was designed to look uncontrolled while being precisely so.

The dust is still in the air when the first Cardassian broadcast begins.

`CUT TO:`

---

`INT. ENTERPRISE-A — BRIDGE — CONTINUOUS`

---

**COMMUNICATIONS OFFICER**
Captain — Cardassian vessel broadcasting on all frequencies. They're claiming —

---

**KIRK**
On screen.

The broadcast. A CARDASSIAN OFFICIAL — smooth, prepared, the specific composure of someone delivering a speech they wrote before the event it describes:

---

**CARDASSIAN OFFICIAL** *(ON SCREEN)*
— unprovoked attack on civilian infrastructure. Federation elements have demonstrated clear hostile intent toward the development mission —

Kirk is already at the tactical station.

---

**KIRK**
*(to Hummer, low)*
What do you have?

Hummer's hands move across the console. The readings from the explosion. The sensor analysis. His eyes moving fast.

---

**HUMMER**
Working.

His console scrolls.

Debris field composition. Energy signature. Blast pattern.

His eyes stop moving.

There — in the debris field readings. A hull composition trace. Faint. Fragmentary. But there.

He knows that trace.

He has seen it before in a different intelligence context — a contractor profile in a Starfleet security assessment. Cardassian military construction specification. Not civilian. Military.

He starts calculating.

Is this reading sufficient cause to invoke the Emergency Disclosure Protocol?

On the screen the Cardassian broadcast continues.

Hummer calculates.

The Cardassian vessel maintains its orbit.

Hummer calculates.

He pulls up the protocol specifications. Cross-references the reading against the evidentiary threshold. The reading is fragmentary. The trace is partial. Is it sufficient? Is it?

He calculates.

The Cardassian vessel begins a routine orbital correction.

He calculates.

The vessel moves.

The sensor geometry shifts.

The thirty-eight second window closes.

He has the reading. He has the analysis. He has nothing that will hold up.

He has a glass of grey water.

He says nothing.

---

**KIRK**
*(still watching the broadcast)*
Hummer. Report.

---

**HUMMER**
*(a beat — a beat too long)*
Explosion in the eastern residential sector. Civilian building. Casualty reports coming in from the surface — Federation citizens among the dead.

*(beat)*

Blast pattern is... I'm still analyzing, sir.

Kirk looks at him.

The specific look of a man who has been in enough tactical situations to know when *still analyzing* means something was there and is now gone.

He says nothing.

Looks back at the screen.

---

**KIRK**
*(to communications)*
Get me Starfleet Command. Priority channel.

Then — separately, quietly, in a voice that doesn't carry past the tactical station:

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
*(to Hummer)*
When you're done analyzing — come see me.

`CUT TO:`

---

*End of Scene 5*
