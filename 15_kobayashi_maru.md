# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 15 — "KOBAYASHI MARU"

---

`EXT. CORDIA IV — SURFACE — DAY`

Kirk materializes in a corridor.

Not a public space. Not a diplomatic venue. A maintenance corridor in a Cardassian-built facility — functional, utilitarian, the specific aesthetic of infrastructure that exists to work rather than to impress.

GUL MAREK is there.

Not the Marek of the diplomatic dinner table. The jacket is the same but the ease is different — tighter, the ease of a man in a private crisis performing composure.

He looks at Kirk the way he looked at Sorak at the dinner table — reading. Assessing whether this is the right person to say the next thing to.

He decided before Kirk arrived.

He hands over a data chip without preamble.

---

**MAREK**
Original construction orders for the northern installation. Classification level four above mine.

I obtained them fourteen days ago from a source I will not name.

Kirk looks at the chip. Then at Marek.

---

**KIRK**
Why?

Marek is quiet for a moment.

He looks at the wall — Cardassian construction, solid, the specific quality of something built to last. Built by his people.

---

**MAREK**
The installation on those documents —

He pauses.

Something happened to his face when he pulled up those files fourteen days ago and it hasn't quite gone back to where it was.

---

**MAREK** *(CONT'D)*
What I was told we were building here — what I believed we were building — I could defend that. I could stand in front of a tribunal and defend that.

*(beat)*

What is actually being built —

He stops.

---

**KIRK**
How large?

---

**MAREK**
Ten times.

Ten times what any of my colleagues know. Ten times what the Klingon alliance was told. Ten times what I believed.

*(beat)*

There is a section of this operation that has been compartmentalized above every officer of my rank. Something planned — and I have been here, Captain. I have been here building it — without knowing what I was building.

The weight of that sits between them.

---

**MAREK** *(CONT'D)*
I remember what Cardassia looked like after the Dominion War.

*(beat)*

I rebuilt something from that. With my own hands. Many of us did.

*(beat)*

The people who authorized this installation — they were not there for the rebuilding. They remember the empire. Not the rubble.

And the Klingons —

He says it with a specific disgust.

---

**MAREK** *(CONT'D)*
They are using us. This installation serves their timeline, not ours. When the war comes — and if this continues, it will come — Cardassia will fight it and the Klingons will watch us bleed and call it strategy.

*(beat)*

I will not let that happen again.

He looks at the chip in Kirk's hand.

---

**MAREK** *(CONT'D)*
What you do with those documents is your problem.

My problem is already solved.

He leaves.

Back down the corridor. Back to his ship. Back to his post.

He calculated the cost of this moment fourteen days ago when he pulled up the files and saw what was actually there.

He paid it then. Not now. Now is just the transaction completing.

---

Kirk stands alone in the corridor.

Holds the chip.

He looks at it for a long moment.

He knows what Sorak needs him to do with this.

He knows the Kobayashi Maru when he's in it.

He starts thinking about the third option.

The messy one. The uncertain one.

The one that leaves the future open.

`CUT TO:`

---

`INT. UNKNOWN LOCATION — CONTINUOUS`

Sorak at his display.

The tactical situation from Cordia system. The installation data. The political landscape.

He is watching Kirk's solution emerge in real time.

Not the solution he built the Kobayashi Maru for.

Something else.

He watches the documentation moving through Federation diplomatic channels — not through his architecture, not through the official chain that leads to the strategic response he planned.

Through the open diplomatic registry. Publicly. In front of every neutral world that has a stake in the Cardassian economic sphere.

In front of every world that has been told the thirty-year investment was humanitarian.

In front of every world that is now reading the original construction orders. The dates. The classification levels. The scope.

Ten times larger than anyone knew.

Ten times larger than Sorak's own intelligence estimated.

He looks at that number for a long moment.

His model had one installation. The corridor. The eighteen-month timeline.

What is actually there is not one installation.

He adjusts his probability models by a great deal more than some small percentage.

Then he watches Kirk's disclosure complete.

The Cardassian position collapsing in diplomatic real time. Neutral worlds withdrawing endorsements. The economic sphere fracturing as each participating world recalculates what else might be cover.

No war.

But the ground of the next thirty years just shifted fundamentally.

Sorak looks at his display for a long time.

Something crosses his face.

Not anger.

Not surprise at Kirk.

Surprise at himself.

At what his model couldn't see. At what fifteen years of intelligence work missed because it was looking at factions and not at people.

At one Cardassian military officer who didn't want this and couldn't be seen in any database as someone who wouldn't want this — because databases don't have that column.

Kirk had no database. Kirk had no model.

Kirk walked into a room and saw a man.

---

He is still looking at his display when the door opens.

Kirk enters.

They look at each other.

The room is quiet.

---

**SORAK**
You didn't use the channel I prepared.

---

**KIRK**
No.

---

**SORAK**
The strategic outcome through my channel would have been — considerably more durable.

---

**KIRK**
Yes.

---

**SORAK**
What you did leaves the situation unresolved. The installation will be challenged through arbitration. That process takes years. The outcome is uncertain. The Cardassians will adapt. They will rebuild their diplomatic position. They will find new approaches.

*(beat)*

You've only postponed the inevitable by thirty years.

Kirk looks at him.

Steady. Clear.

---

**KIRK**
It's up to the next generation to postpone by another thirty.

Silence.

Sorak looks at Kirk.

For a long moment he says nothing.

He says nothing for long enough that the saying of nothing becomes the most eloquent thing he has said in fifteen years.

Which is the most Sorak has ever admitted anyone was right about anything.

`CUT TO:`

---

*End of Scene 15*
