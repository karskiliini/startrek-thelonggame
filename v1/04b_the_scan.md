# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 4B — "THE SCAN"


INT. ENTERPRISE-A — BRIEFING ROOM


Spock at the head of the table. Kirk. McCoy. Hummer. The senior staff briefing Kirk ordered when they arrived.

Spock brings up a tactical display of the asteroid belt. The image rotates slowly — the vast field of rock, the three mining sectors carved into it, the transit corridors between them.


                         SPOCK
          The belt survey is complete. Three findings of note.

He highlights a cluster of asteroids in the inner ring. They glow amber on the display — a colour that means heat where there should be none.


                         SPOCK (CONT'D)
          First. Thermal anomalies in seven asteroids within the Klingon mining sector. The heat signatures are consistent with subsurface installation — power generation, atmospheric processing. They are not consistent with mining operations.

He touches the display. Transit lines appear — threads of light tracing paths through the belt.


                         SPOCK (CONT'D)
          Second. Cargo transit volume through the inner ring has increased by a factor of three in eighteen months. The material profile indicates construction-grade alloys. The destination signatures converge on the same seven asteroids.

               (beat)

          Third. The installations appear to be at varying stages of completion. The largest is approximately seventy percent operational based on power output modelling.

Silence.


                         KIRK
          What are they building?


                         SPOCK
          I cannot determine the purpose from orbit. The mineral composition of the surrounding rock degrades sensor resolution below useful thresholds at this range. A closer survey would be required.


                         KIRK
          How close?


                         SPOCK
          Inside the belt.

Kirk looks at the display. Seven amber dots in a field of grey stone.


                         BONES
          Inside the belt. In a shuttle. With a Klingon warbird parked on top of it.


                         KIRK
          Noted.

               (to the room)

          We continue scanning from orbit. Full spectral analysis on those seven signatures. I want to know what's in there before we decide how close we need to get.


The briefing ends. The officers file out. Kirk stays, looking at the display. Seven dots. Seven questions.


CUT TO:


INT. IKS REK'THAR — BRIDGE


K'Tagh is standing at the tactical display. He has been watching Enterprise since she arrived. He watches the way a predator watches — not the movement, but the attention. Where Enterprise points her sensors tells him what she knows.

Enterprise's sensor cone has shifted. It was scanning the colony. Now it is scanning the belt.

The Klingon mining sector.

His sector.

K'Tagh stares at the display for a long time. The particular stillness of a man who is calculating the cost of what he is about to do.


                         K'TAGH
               (to his communications officer — quiet, the voice that his crew fears more than the shouting)
          Private channel. Surface frequency. Encrypted.

The channel opens. K'Tagh speaks in Klingon. We don't hear the words — the camera stays on his face. The face of a man giving an order he cannot take back.

He closes the channel.

Then he turns to his first officer.


                         K'TAGH
               (in Federation Standard — for the audience)
          If anyone asks — Klingons do not use mining tools as weapons. It is beneath us. If we wanted to attack the Cardassian settlement, we would use the disruptor banks on this ship. In daylight. With our name on it.

               (beat)

          Remember that.

The first officer says nothing. He understands. The order was given. The deniability has been constructed. Whatever happens on the surface in the next few hours — the Rek'thar was in orbit, weapons powered down, with no reason to fire.

K'Tagh returns to his display. Enterprise is still scanning the belt.

Not for long.
