# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 8 — "READY ROOM"

---

`INT. ENTERPRISE-A — CAPTAIN'S READY ROOM — MOMENTS LATER`

Hummer sits across from Kirk.

The door is closed. Kirk is standing.

He is not angry.

That is, in its way, worse.

---

**KIRK**
How did you get here, Hummer?

---

**HUMMER**
Sir?

---

**KIRK**
Not what you did. How you got here. To this chair.

He gestures — not the ready room chair specifically. The tactical station. The uniform. The whole distance from Vega Colony to here.

---

**HUMMER**
Hard work. Testing well. The training program —

---

**KIRK**
You got here by never being wrong.

*(beat)*

That's different.

Hummer is quiet.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
Every step. Every evaluation. Every assignment. You waited until you were certain before you moved. And it worked. I understand why it worked. A man in your position couldn't afford to be wrong. Being wrong meant going back. So you made sure you were never wrong.

He's not accusing. He's diagnosing. There's a difference and Hummer feels it.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
The problem is you brought that habit onto my bridge.

---

**HUMMER**
The reading was fragmentary. The protocol threshold —

---

**KIRK**
What was your first instinct?

Hummer stops.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
In the first fourteen seconds. Before the calculation. Before the cross-reference. What did you know?

A long silence.

When Hummer answers, it's very quiet.

---

**HUMMER**
That it was Cardassian military construction.

---

**KIRK**
And you waited.

---

**HUMMER**
I wasn't certain.

---

**KIRK**
You were certain enough.

The silence between them has weight.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
I need you to act before you're certain. I need you to move when your gut says move. Not when your calculation says it's safe to move.

*(beat)*

If you act and you're wrong — I won't hold it against you. Wrong that moves fast I can recover from.

He looks at Hummer directly.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
But if you hesitate. If you wait for certainty in a moment that has no luxury of certainty — and that hesitation costs this ship something it cannot get back —

A beat.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
Then we're done. Not because you were wrong. Because you were late.

Hummer sits with the paradox. He's smart enough to see exactly what was just built around him.

Kirk moves to the door. Hand on the frame. Almost to himself:

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
You've spent your whole career making sure you deserved to be here.

He looks back.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
You deserve to be here. Now stop proving it and just be here.

He leaves.

Hummer sits alone in the ready room for a moment.

He looks like a man who has seen himself clearly for the first time.

It is not comfortable.

It is the ignition.
