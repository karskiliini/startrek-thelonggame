# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 10 — "THE CANYON"

---

`INT. ENTERPRISE-A — BRIDGE — LATER`

The canyon below.

On the viewer — Kerata's moon, the dark wound of the canyon system visible.

The situation has been pushed there by degrees — small incidents, positioning moves, the Cardassian vessel claiming sensor interference that requires them to move closer to the moon while the installation on the surface continues.

Nobody chose this directly. The situation chose it.

Or someone arranged for the situation to.

Either way — here.

Dense ionized rock. Sensor range forty kilometers. Communications degrading.

The Klingon warbird somewhere in the ring, cloaked, position unknown.

The Cardassian vessel moving, slowly, toward the corridor entrance.

The installation advancing on the surface.

The clock running.

KIRK stands.

He and SPOCK exchange a look. Quiet. Private.

---

**KIRK**
*(low — not for the bridge)*
The surface. Marek. I need to talk to him before this closes completely.

---

**SPOCK**
You believe he will cooperate.

---

**KIRK**
I believe he brought a family heirloom to a starbase dinner and cooked for three people he doesn't trust. That's not a man who's comfortable with what's cooking.

*(beat)*

And if I'm wrong — I need to know that too.

---

**SPOCK**
The timing is — suboptimal.

---

**KIRK**
It always is.

He looks at his bridge. His crew. The tactical station.

HUMMER.

Hummer who has been quieter since the ready room conversation. Working. Listening to everything. Running calculations that he then sets aside and looks at the console with instead of into it.

Rebuilding.

Kirk walks to the tactical station. Stands next to Hummer. Looking at the same readouts.

---

**HUMMER**
*(without looking up)*
The Klingon ship has been running silent for forty minutes. They're in the ring somewhere. I've been trying to establish a probable position from drift analysis.

*(beat)*

Two candidates.

He puts them on screen. Two zones in the asteroid ring. Either one is where a Klingon commander following standard doctrine would hold.

Kirk looks at the zones. Then at Hummer.

He doesn't say anything about the ready room.

He doesn't say anything about what's coming.

He just looks at his tactical officer for one moment — the look of a man placing a bet he has calculated carefully.

Then, clearly, for the bridge:

---

**KIRK**
You have the conn.

And walks to the turbolift.

The doors close.

The bridge is quiet.

A LIEUTENANT COMMANDER at the helm — senior to Hummer by two grades — turns in his seat.

Says nothing.

Just looks at the tactical station.

Hummer doesn't look back at him. He's already reading.

---

**HUMMER**
*(quiet. Settled. The voice of someone who has arrived somewhere they've been traveling toward)*
Maintain current position. Passive sensors only. I want everything we have on those two zones.

He looks at his console.

Then — something.

Something in the way the rock is moving. Something in the drift pattern that his hands learned before his mind did.

He knows these rocks.

---

**HUMMER** *(CONT'D)*
*(very quietly, to himself)*
There you are.

He sits forward.

Begins.

`CUT TO:`

*End of Scene 9*
