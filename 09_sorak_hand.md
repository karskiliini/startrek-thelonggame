# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 9 — "SORAK'S HAND"

---

`INT. UNKNOWN LOCATION — CONTINUOUS`

Not a Starfleet facility. No insignia. No official architecture. The kind of room that exists in the administrative gap between what the Federation funds and what it needs.

SORAK stands at a display.

The display shows the Kerata system. The explosion site. The Cardassian broadcast transcript. The tactical record from Enterprise's bridge — which he should not have access to and which he has had access to for the last forty minutes.

An AIDE stands near the door.

---

**AIDE**
The protocol window closed without action.

---

**SORAK**
I know.

---

**AIDE**
Does that affect the timeline?

Sorak studies the display.

---

**SORAK**
Kirk is dealing with it. He'll get his bridge in order.

*(beat)*

The Cardassians will interpret the failure to invoke as confirmation that their cover is holding. They'll accelerate.

---

**AIDE**
Is that — is that a problem?

Sorak looks at the Kerata system on the display.

---

**SORAK**
It's a variable.

He moves to another console. Pulls up a different display — personnel files, assignment rosters, ship positioning. The architecture of fifteen years of careful preparation.

---

**AIDE**
If the installation completes before we can act —

---

**SORAK**
It won't complete.

He's looking at a specific file. Kirk's file. Open. Annotated in the margins with observations that go back years.

---

**SORAK** *(CONT'D)*
Kirk won't need the protocol. He'll find something the protocol would never have given him.

*(beat)*

I've arranged for that to be possible.

He looks at the tactical record from Enterprise's bridge. The moment the window closed. Hummer at the console, running the calculation again.

---

**SORAK** *(CONT'D)*
*(very quietly, to himself)*
Get your bridge in order, Captain.

He closes the file.

Outside — somewhere in the Kerata system — things are accelerating.
