# STAR TREK: THE LONG GAME
## Scene 12 — "ANYONE'S TO WIN"

---

`INT. ENTERPRISE-A — CAPTAIN'S READY ROOM — LATER`

Aftermath.

The political settlement imperfect, unresolved, alive. The Cardassian installation exists but its legal status is contested — formally under Federation arbitration, practically a slow negotiation that will take years. Nobody won cleanly. The grey water is still grey.

Kirk opens a private channel.

K'TAGH appears on screen. Behind him — his vessel, preparing to depart the system.

---

**K'TAGH**
Captain.

---

**KIRK**
Commander.

A beat. Formal on the surface. Everything acknowledged underneath.

---

**K'TAGH**
The corridor remains open.

---

**KIRK**
For now.

---

**K'TAGH**
For now.

She looks at him for a moment. Two commanders who saw each other clearly across a dark canyon and chose not to pull the trigger.

---

**K'TAGH** *(CONT'D)*
Your tactical officer.

---

**KIRK**
What about him.

---

**K'TAGH**
He knew where we'd be. Before we knew.

*(beat)*

Give him a ship, Captain. Before someone with less judgment than you makes the decision for you.

She closes the channel first.

Kirk looks at the blank screen for a moment.

Lets it sit.

`CUT TO:`

---

`INT. STARFLEET COMMAND — REVIEW BOARD — DAY`

A formal room. A long table. Five officers.

ADMIRAL VOSS at the center.

The file in front of each of them belongs to Hummer.

KIRK stands to one side. Not at the table. Present as the recommending officer.

BOARD MEMBER CHEN — earnest, procedural — has the hesitation incident open.

---

**CHEN**
The Emergency Disclosure window closed without action. The incident is on record. It cost the operation its primary evidentiary basis at a critical juncture.

*(to Kirk)*

Captain. Given this record — the hesitation under pressure, the failure to act within the required window — can you speak to this officer's suitability for independent command?

Kirk looks at the board. Then at Hummer's file. Then at the board again.

---

**KIRK**
I've seen officers freeze under pressure and never recover.

*(beat)*

I've seen officers who never froze but never truly led either. Who performed correctly in every situation because they never took a risk that might produce an incorrect performance.

*(beat)*

This officer froze once. He understood exactly why. And then he did something most officers never manage.

He learned to trust himself more than his fear.

In the same engagement — the same engagement where the hesitation incident occurred — he acted on instinct before confirmation, found the instinct incomplete mid-execution, and corrected course without losing a single second of momentum.

He gave orders he couldn't yet confirm were right because he was already thinking three moves past them.

He looks at the board.

---

**KIRK** *(CONT'D)*
That is not a liability.

That is the only quality that matters when everything else fails.

*(beat)*

Give him a ship.

Silence in the room.

VOSS has been looking at Kirk throughout this speech.

Now she looks at Hummer's file.

She thinks about a dining room on a starbase. Cardassian wine. A man who was charming and cultured and absolutely certain the Federation would not look closely enough.

She thinks about the thirty-eight second window.

She thinks about what was in the documents. Ten times larger than anyone estimated. Ten times larger than anything she told Sorak was sufficient evidence to act on.

She picks up her PADD.

Signs the recommendation.

No comment. No acknowledgment that anything was wrong or right or different from what she expected.

She wasn't wrong about anything. The lead did get away. That is also true.

She signs it.

`CUT TO:`

---

`INT. ENTERPRISE-A — BRIDGE — LATER`

The ship is quiet. The post-crisis quiet of a vessel still counting the cost.

KIRK in the command chair. McCOY beside him. Neither looking at the other. Just the viewer. Stars.

McCoy pours two glasses of water. Hands one to Kirk. Holds his own up to the light.

Grey.

---

**McCOY**
Still foggy.

---

**KIRK**
At least it's not red.

McCoy glances at him. Kirk doesn't look back. Neither of them smiles.

McCoy drinks the grey water.

Kirk drinks the grey water.

`CUT TO:`

---

`INT. USS CENTAUR — BRIDGE — LATER`

Small. Slightly battered. The specific dignity of a vessel that knows what it is and doesn't apologize.

HUMMER walks onto the bridge.

His bridge.

The crew turns. Young faces, mostly. A lieutenant commander at the helm who looks like she got here the same way Hummer did — freight hauler, probably. Something in the way she stands.

Hummer looks at them.

Looks at the viewscreen.

Stars. And somewhere in them — the future. Unresolved. Arguable. Still anyone's to write.

He goes to the command chair.

Sits.

Reaches — almost without thinking — for the water dispenser built into the chair's armrest.

Pours a glass.

Holds it to the light.

Grey. Slightly cloudy.

He looks at it for a moment.

He drinks it.

Turns to his console.

Begins.

`FADE TO BLACK.`

---

> **THE FUTURE IS ANYONE'S TO WIN.**

`FADE OUT.`
